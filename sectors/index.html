Federal Government

Pebble Canada engages Government of Canada departments and agencies through structured procurement processes aligned with PSPC policy, Treasury Board Standards, and departmental compliance frameworks. Our delivery model is built for the scrutiny that public sector engagements demand. We are registered on CanadaBuys / SAP Ariba, understand ATIP requirements, and maintain audit-ready documentation across all projects.

We are closely aligned with the Government of Canada's Major Projects Office (MPO), the coordinating body advancing Projects of National Interest under the Building Canada Act. These nation-building initiatives — spanning nuclear energy, critical minerals, and strategic trade infrastructure — represent over $60 billion in federal investment, and Pebble Canada is positioned to support their delivery across all four service pillars.

Critical Minerals & Mining

Canada's strategic position as a top-tier critical minerals supplier creates significant delivery requirements across workforce mobilization, logistics, and operational technology. Pebble Canada supports the full value chain from extraction through processing and logistics. We have deep expertise in remote operations, fly-in/fly-out workforce models, and the regulatory complexities of mining in Canadian jurisdictions.

Several major critical minerals facilities have been designated — or are candidates for designation — as Projects of National Interest under the Major Projects Office. The Nouveau Monde Graphite Matawinie Mine in Québec is among the highest-profile examples — a large-scale graphite mine requiring extensive workforce and logistics support during construction and operations. Pebble Canada is structured to serve exactly this type of deployment.

Designated Projects of National Interest under the Building Canada Act receive dedicated federal coordination — accelerated permitting, intergovernmental alignment, and streamlined regulatory engagement — to unlock over $60 billion in national investment.

Pebble Canada operates across the precise sectors that host Canada's most consequential projects: nuclear energy, critical minerals, trade infrastructure, and defence. Our service model is structured for the scale, compliance demands, and multi-stakeholder complexity that these projects require.
