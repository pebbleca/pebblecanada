Defence Industrial Base

Positioned Within Canada's Defence Procurement Ecosystem

Canada's defence investment is accelerating, driven by the commitment to reach 2% GDP in NATO defence spending and a DND capital investment plan exceeding $38 billion over the coming years. The majority of this spend flows through CanadaBuys and PSPC procurement channels, within a Defence Industrial Base framework that prioritizes Canadian industrial participation, technology sovereignty, and supply chain integrity. Pebble Canada is registered and procurement-ready to compete within this ecosystem.

Effective participation in defence procurement requires more than vendor registration. It demands familiarity with PSPC's contracting policy framework, compliance with the Controlled Goods Program for sensitive technology, and the ability to produce technical bids that meet DND's evaluation criteria. Pebble Canada's procurement infrastructure, including compliance matrices, technical submissions, and verified supply chains, is built to the standards that competitive defence solicitations require.

Controlled Goods & Security Compliance

Work involving controlled technology, dual-use equipment, or sensitive programme data requires adherence to the Controlled Goods Program and, where applicable, PSPC's Industrial Security Programme. Pebble Canada's compliance posture, including our in-progress ISP registration, is designed to meet these obligations and to support clients operating in regulated programme environments.

Competitive Procurement & Bid Capability

DND and PSPC solicitations reward suppliers who can demonstrate technical depth, compliance readiness, and delivery credibility, not just price. Pebble Canada approaches competitive procurements with structured bid development: evaluation-criterion mapping, compliance matrix preparation, technical narrative, and delivery schedule documentation aligned to statement-of-work requirements.
